Privacy Policy

Your privacy and trust are paramount to us. This policy explains how SOMDA collects, uses, and protects your personal information.

Last updated: January 2025

Introduction

SOMDA ("we," "us," "our," or "Company") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains our data practices and how we handle information you provide to us through our website, services, and interactions. We understand the sensitive nature of the cases we handle and take extraordinary measures to safeguard your personal information.

This Privacy Policy applies to all individuals who visit our website, use our services, or interact with SOMDA in any capacity. By accessing our website or using our services, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy and agree to its terms.

If you do not agree with our privacy practices, please do not use our services or visit our website. We encourage you to read this policy carefully and contact us if you have any questions or concerns about our privacy practices.

Information We Collect

Information You Provide Directly

We collect information that you voluntarily provide to us through various means, including:

  • Contact forms on our website
  • Email correspondence and communications
  • Consultation requests and intake forms
  • Phone calls and voice messages
  • In-person meetings and consultations
  • Legal retainer agreements and contracts
  • Case files and supporting documentation
  • Payment and billing information

This information may include your name, email address, phone number, physical address, date of birth, identification numbers, financial information, and details about your legal matter or case. In cases involving gender-based violence or family conflict, you may provide sensitive personal information about your circumstances, safety concerns, family members, and other parties involved.

Information Collected Automatically

When you visit our website, we may automatically collect certain information about your device and browsing activities:

  • IP address and device identifiers
  • Browser type and operating system
  • Pages visited and time spent on our website
  • Referring website or source
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies
  • Clickstream data and navigation patterns
  • Search queries and form submissions

We use this information to improve our website, understand user behavior, enhance security, and provide you with a better experience. This data is typically aggregated and does not identify you personally unless combined with other information.

Information from Third Parties

In some circumstances, we may receive information about you from third parties, including:

  • Court records and legal documents
  • Law enforcement and government agencies
  • Other legal professionals and attorneys
  • Referral sources and support organizations
  • Background check and investigative services
  • Public records and databases
  • Social media and online platforms

We collect this information only as necessary to provide legal services, conduct investigations, or fulfill our professional obligations. All third-party information is handled with the same level of confidentiality and security as information you provide directly.

How We Use Your Information

SOMDA uses the information we collect for the following purposes:

Legal Services and Case Management

We use your information to provide legal representation, conduct investigations, prepare legal documents, communicate with courts, and manage your case. This is the primary purpose for collecting your personal information.

Communication and Client Support

We use your contact information to communicate with you about your case, provide updates, schedule appointments, and offer support services. We may contact you via email, phone, or mail.

Billing and Payment Processing

We use financial information to process payments, invoice clients, and manage billing records. This information is handled securely and in compliance with applicable payment processing standards.

Website Improvement and Analytics

We analyze website usage data to improve our online services, enhance user experience, and optimize our website's functionality and performance.

Legal Compliance and Safety

We use information as required by law, to comply with court orders, to protect our legal rights, and to ensure the safety and security of our clients and staff.

Research and Quality Improvement

With your consent, we may use anonymized information for research purposes, case outcome analysis, and continuous improvement of our services.

Marketing and Service Announcements

We may use your contact information to send you information about our services, legal updates, or important announcements. You can opt out of marketing communications at any time.

Information Sharing and Disclosure

SOMDA maintains strict confidentiality regarding client information. We do not sell, rent, or lease your personal information to third parties. However, we may share your information in the following limited circumstances:

Legal and Court Requirements

We may disclose information when required by law, court order, subpoena, or government authority. We will notify you of such requests when legally permitted to do so.

Service Providers and Contractors

We may share information with trusted service providers, investigators, expert witnesses, and other professionals who assist us in providing legal services. These parties are bound by confidentiality obligations.

Co-Counsel and Legal Collaboration

When necessary for your case, we may share information with other attorneys or legal professionals assisting with your representation.

Safety and Protection

We may disclose information when necessary to protect your safety, our safety, or the safety of others, or to prevent fraud or illegal activity.

Your Consent

We may share information with other organizations or individuals when you provide explicit consent for such sharing.

Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience and gather information about website usage. Cookies are small text files stored on your device that help us remember your preferences and understand how you interact with our site.

Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential Cookies: Required for website functionality and security
  • Performance Cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our website
  • Preference Cookies: Remember your settings and preferences
  • Marketing Cookies: Track your interactions for targeted advertising

Managing Cookies

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through browser settings. You can choose to accept or reject cookies, or be notified when cookies are being sent. However, disabling cookies may affect certain website features and functionality. For more information about managing cookies, visit www.allaboutcookies.org.

Third-Party Tracking

Our website may contain links to third-party websites and services. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these external sites. We encourage you to review their privacy policies before providing any personal information.

Data Security and Protection

SOMDA takes data security seriously and implements comprehensive measures to protect your personal information from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. Our security practices include:

  • Encrypted data transmission using SSL/TLS protocols
  • Secure data storage with access controls and authentication
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments
  • Employee training on data protection and confidentiality
  • Strict access policies limiting information to authorized personnel
  • Backup systems and disaster recovery procedures
  • Incident response protocols for potential data breaches
  • Compliance with applicable data protection regulations

While we employ robust security measures, no system is completely secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security of your information. You are responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of any passwords or access credentials you use to access our services.

Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information:

  • Right to Access: You may request access to the personal information we hold about you
  • Right to Correction: You may request that we correct inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Right to Deletion: You may request deletion of your information, subject to legal and professional obligations
  • Right to Opt-Out: You may opt out of marketing communications and certain data processing activities
  • Right to Portability: You may request your information in a portable format
  • Right to Object: You may object to certain types of data processing

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the information provided in the Contact Information section below. We will respond to your request within the timeframe required by applicable law.

Data Retention

SOMDA retains personal information for as long as necessary to provide legal services, comply with legal obligations, and resolve any disputes. Retention periods vary depending on the type of information and its purpose:

  • Client case files are retained for the duration of representation plus any required post-case period
  • Legal documents and court records are maintained in accordance with applicable legal requirements
  • Billing and financial records are retained for accounting and tax purposes
  • Website analytics and cookies are typically retained for up to 24 months
  • Email communications may be retained for professional and legal reasons

After the retention period expires, information is securely deleted or anonymized. However, some information may be retained longer if required by law or professional standards.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

SOMDA may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors. We will notify you of material changes by posting the updated policy on our website and updating the "Last Updated" date. Your continued use of our website or services after such modifications constitutes your acceptance of the updated Privacy Policy.

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy regularly to stay informed about how we protect your information. If you do not agree with any changes, you should discontinue use of our website and services.

Additional Information

Children's Privacy

Our website and services are not directed to individuals under 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that we have collected information from a child without parental consent, we will take steps to delete such information immediately.

International Data Transfers

If you are located outside our primary jurisdiction, please be aware that your information may be transferred to, stored in, and processed in countries other than your country of residence. By using our services, you consent to such transfers.

Links to Third-Party Websites

Our website may contain links to third-party websites. This Privacy Policy applies only to our website. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of linked websites. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party sites before providing personal information.

California Privacy Rights

If you are a California resident, you may have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). For more information about your California privacy rights, please contact us at the information provided above.
